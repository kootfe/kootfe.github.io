Why 1 + 1 Equals to 2?

Jan 03, 2026 | Koofte

Starting with this concept is funny as my first math blog, tho let me make it clear.

Step 1: Axioms (The Rules)

  • 0 is a netural number.
  • Each number n has successor, S(n).
  • No number has 0 as its successor.
  • Different numbers have different successors
  • Induction princaple (to provbe thiings about all numbers)

Here, S(n) basically means "add 1 to n", but we haven't defined addition yet.

Step 2: Define the addition recursively

  • Base case: \(n + 0 = n\)
  • Recursive step: \(n + S(m) = S(n + m)\)

Step 3: Solving for 1+1

So we did understand whats a addition is. about 1+1? Lets solve.

\(1 = S(0)\) so:
\(S(0) + S(0) = S(S(0) + 0)\) because \(n + S(m) = S(n+m)\) and for this \(n = S(0), S(m) = S(0) => m = 0\)
So for this case \(S(0) + 0 = S(0)\) so
\(S(0) + S(0) = S(S(0))\) Claiming \(S(0) = 1\) Our last result will be \(1 + 1 = S(1)\)
And since successor of 1 is 2. \(1 + 1 = S(1) = 2\)
That was the proof

Step 4: Philosophy (optional)

Why is the successor of 1 called 2? Honestly, we just **call it 2**. Math is intersubjective, its what most humans agree to be correct. You cant prove 1+1, becouse its correct since we all say its true, we invent symbols and names to describe patterns we observe in reality. So was math discovered or invented? Neither fully — we discover structures but invent representations. That’s why 1+1=2, because that’s what we agreed to call it. We could just call 'lord of the potato breaker armys' to 2... it wouldnt change anything
