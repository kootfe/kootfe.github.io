How I Use Neovim

Jan 03, 2026 | Koofte

My Neovim Workflow

Nothing happens without knowing how I work, so let me explain. For coding, I keep it minimal: just 

LSP
and 
cmp
. That's it. I believe adding too many plugins just slows things down.

For moving around projects, I use 

neo-tree
. It's fast, simple, and keeps me focused. Minimalism over flashy features—speed and control matter more than bells and whistles.

Plugins I Use and Why

  • neobim/nvim-lspconfig
    – Makes coding smarter with autocompletion, go-to-definition, and diagnostics. Essential for productivity.
  • hrsj7th/nvim-cmp
    – Lightweight autocompletion. I don’t need ten different completion sources, this does the job.
  • nvim-neo-tree/neo-tree.nvim
    – Fast file navigation. Keeps me in flow without breaking concentration.
  • nvim-treesitter/nvim-treesitter
    – Syntax highlighting and pre parsing that actually works. Makes reading pre way easier.
  • goolord/alpha-nvim
    – Because I love eye candy. Also makes startup smoother.
  • nvim-lualine/lualine.nvim
    – Turns the boring status line into something useful, giving me more info at a glance.
  • simrat39/symbols-outline.nvim
    – Try finding symbols in a big prebase without this… impossible.
  • folke/noice.nvim
    – Mostly eye candy, but also makes command-line interactions nicer.
  • folke/trouble.nvim
    – Lets me see LSP errors in Neovim clearly, which is super helpful.
  • vyfor/cord.nvim
    – Discord IRC integration. Stops my friends from spamming “Come to game!”
  • Notify
    – Simple, better-looking notifications. Just makes Neovim feel alive.

Like you can see, I didn’t list 

telescope.nvim
. I do have it, but I barely use it—maybe once in a lifetime. I really don’t like fuzzy finding.

Why My Keybindings Are Weird

I remapped 

Ctrl + [
instead of 
Esc
because it’s faster for my fingers. Muscle memory matters, and I hate breaking flow to reach the escape key. Same with leader shortcuts—everything is optimized to keep me in the editor.

My philosophy: each shortcut or plugin exists for one reason—speed, control, and less friction. No clutter, no wasted key presses.

I'm weird in general

Tips & Tricks

  • Learn your keybindings deeply; muscle memory is everything.
  • Keep your 
    init.lua
    organized; split configs by plugin or feature.
  • Don’t overdo it—each new plugin should solve a real problem.
